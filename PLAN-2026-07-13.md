This file is the current execution plan for July 16–21. `GOAL.md` owns the product promise and current state; `AGENTS.md` owns operating rules; `log.md` is the append-only evidence record. Older chronology from the July 13 runbook is superseded by this plan.

## Outcome

Ship a local, polished professional workbench that turns messy thinking and selected Sources into deliverables a serious professional would send:

**voice or text → grounded visual Map → approved Brief → branded presentation and supporting Outputs → approved Storyboard → narrated Video**

NotebookLM is the reference for clarity, source grounding, and low-friction orientation. WorkshopLM's differentiation is delivery: editable, source-defensible, on-brand work rather than terminal study artifacts.

The public demo video is the primary judge experience. The local fixture and plugin provide truthful, repeatable supporting proof; judges are not expected to recreate the project.

## Current product truth

- The product runs locally in the Codex in-app browser. Do not add hosting, a separate account system, cloud storage, or a remote worker for the hackathon.
- WorkshopLM owns its center Conversation experience. It accepts text and has a Realtime voice path; it is not dependent on native ChatGPT task or voice-turn synchronization.
- Sources are local and deterministically grounded through normalization, stable locators, FTS5/BM25, exact search, and shared `search`/`fetch` tools. Hosted File Search remains optional.
- The professional path is **Capture → Shape → Deliver**, with exactly two consequential sign-offs: Brief and Storyboard.
- The workbench has one current object, persistent-but-quiet Sources and Production context, progressive disclosure, and no persistent tab maze.
- The official **Apps in ChatGPT · OpenAI Official** library is the locked component and style language. New WorkshopLM composites must be built only from its primitives and documented in `DESIGN.md`.
- The output wedge is the grounded, branded, editable presentation. Infographic, Audio Overview, coherent images, Storyboard, and Video support that wedge.
- Local HyperFrames is the primary Video renderer. FFmpeg remains the narrow fallback; no HyperFrames API/auth work is needed.
- GPT-5.6 operation routing is evidence-driven. The July 16 benchmark selects Terra for grounded Map generation because it matched the deterministic Sol quality score with materially lower latency and token use. Sol remains a quality challenger; Luna remains the latency-oriented repeated-work option.
- Narration and Realtime speech use the Cedar voice. Every generated-voice surface retains explicit AI-voice disclosure.
- Daniel authorized up to **$50 total OpenAI API spend** for this build. Sample text/TTS is approved until the authentic founder brainstorm is recorded July 16. `/feedback` is deliberately deferred until the product and demo are ready.

## Autonomous operating rule

Daniel is not the routine approval bottleneck. The primary integrator decides reversible product, UI, copy, architecture, prompt, routing, recovery, and compatible contract details from evidence, then records material decisions in `GOAL.md` and `log.md`.

Do not stop between authorized increments. Stop only when continuing requires:

- spend that could exceed the authorized $50 ceiling;
- missing credentials with no safe fallback;
- an irreversible external or public action;
- a material privacy, security, licensing, or destructive-data decision;
- a breaking change to the locked product promise;
- removal of an objective-critical capability after both its primary path and designed fallback fail with recorded evidence.

Do not reread the entire historical log or every research file before acting. Read `GOAL.md`, the latest relevant log entries, the affected code, and only the specific design/research references needed for the current increment. Rendered UI and verified runtime behavior outrank stale prose.

Use at most four concurrent slots: one integrator plus three lanes with exclusive paths. The integrator owns shared files, contracts, acceptance, `GOAL.md`, `log.md`, and merges. A red end-to-end seam pauses breadth until green.

## Design quality bar

The interface must feel like a native ChatGPT/Codex workbench: calm, spacious, direct, and confident.

Every material screen must pass the five-second test. A first-time professional can identify:

1. what they are looking at;
2. which Sources support it;
3. its current status;
4. the one best next action.

Enforce these rules:

- No persistent tab maze, dashboard grid, or competing navigation system.
- One dominant current object and one dominant action.
- Sources remain understandable and accessible without dominating the canvas.
- Production state remains visible but quiet.
- Use sheets, focused views, contextual actions, and progressive disclosure instead of exposing the whole feature set at once.
- Use short human labels. Remove internal IDs, file names, implementation terms, duplicate actions, and explanatory copy that exists only to compensate for unclear hierarchy.
- Prefer whitespace, typography, alignment, and scale over cards, borders, badges, and decoration.
- Preserve source trace, version history, `Needs update`, recovery, and all implemented capability while simplifying its presentation.
- Render and inspect desktop and compact states after every material composition change. Passing DOM tests without visual inspection is insufficient.
- Judge every Output by **Would a serious professional send this under their own name?** Functional, generated, or technically compliant is not enough.
