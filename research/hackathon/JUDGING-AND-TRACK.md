# Judging and Work & Productivity Track

## Exact category name

The live Devpost submission-form option is:

> **Work & Productivity**

The rules describe this track as tools that make teams faster or more effective, including workflow automation, customer support, analytics, sales, and back-office operations.

## Category decision

The product is now exclusively professional/work focused. Submit under **Work & Productivity**. Education/Learning mode has been removed from the product scope and demo story.

Strategically, Work & Productivity also creates a cleaner contrast with NotebookLM: the project applies source-grounded understanding to accountable team deliverables and coordinated creative production rather than presenting itself as another study workspace.

## Judging criteria

The four published criteria are equally weighted:

1. **Technological Implementation** — How thoroughly and skillfully the project uses Codex; whether the code reflects genuine effort and a working, non-trivial implementation.
2. **Design** — Whether the project is working or runnable and provides a complete, coherent product experience rather than only a technical proof of concept.
3. **Potential Impact** — Whether the project makes a credible, specific case for solving a real problem for a real audience, and whether the demonstrated solution addresses it.
4. **Quality of the Idea** — Creativity and demonstrated understanding of the problem space.

The formal rules describe a stage-one pass/fail viability check followed by the equally weighted scoring stage. In a tie, the first listed criterion is considered first, so Technological Implementation is the first tie-break signal.

## Practical scoring interpretation

- **Technology:** Show Codex and GPT-5.6 doing important, inspectable work—not appearing only in the README.
- **Design:** Prioritize the usable end-to-end flow, coherent interaction model, responsive states, errors, and believable outputs.
- **Impact:** Use a sharply defined team workflow and demonstrate saved time or improved quality.
- **Idea:** Explain what existing products solve well, the specific gap, and why the chosen OpenAI capabilities make a materially better workflow possible.
