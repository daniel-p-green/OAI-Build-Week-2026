# OpenAI Build Week Rules Digest

Source: https://openai.devpost.com/rules  
Devpost hackathon ID: `30223`  
Slug: `openai`

## Dates

- Registration: July 9, 2026 at 10:00 AM PT through July 21, 2026 at 5:00 PM PT.
- Submission period: July 13, 2026 at 9:00 AM PT through July 21, 2026 at 5:00 PM PT.
- Judging period: July 22, 2026 at 10:00 AM PT through August 5, 2026 at 5:00 PM PT.
- Winners announced: on or around August 12, 2026 at 2:00 PM PT.

## Eligibility

The hackathon is open to eligible individuals who are at least the age of majority where they live, teams of eligible individuals, and eligible organizations in countries and territories that support OpenAI API access, subject to the exclusions in the official rules.

For a team or organization entry, one authorized representative must act and submit on its behalf. The United States appears in the included-country list returned by Devpost.

Always consult the full eligibility section before adding teammates or changing the submitter entity.

## What must be built

- A project built with **Codex and GPT-5.6**.
- It must fit one of the four categories; ours is **Work & Productivity**.
- It must install and run consistently on its intended platform.
- It must function as depicted in the demo video and project description.

## New and pre-existing work

- Projects may be newly created during the submission period.
- A pre-existing project must be meaningfully extended using Codex and/or GPT-5.6 after the submission period begins.
- Only work added during the submission period is evaluated for a pre-existing project.
- Pre-existing projects must clearly distinguish old and new work using evidence such as dated commits, timestamped Codex sessions, or equivalent records.

## Third-party integrations and assets

- We must be authorized to use every third-party SDK, API, data source, and integration.
- The demo may not use third-party trademarks, copyrighted music, or other copyrighted material without permission.
- Open-source components are allowed when their licenses are followed and the submission meaningfully builds on them.
- The submission must be original, owned by the entrant/team/organization, and must not violate intellectual-property, contract, privacy, or publicity rights.

## Testing access

- Judges must receive a working website, functioning demo, or test build.
- The project must be available free of charge and without restrictions for judging and testing through the end of the judging period.
- If access is private, testing instructions must include any required credentials.
- Judges are not required to test the project and may judge only from the description, images, and video. The submission package therefore has to stand on its own.

## Multiple submissions

Multiple submissions are allowed, but each must be unique and substantially different from the others.

## Intellectual property after submission

The entrant retains ownership. The sponsor receives a non-exclusive license for judging, and the sponsor and Devpost may promote the submission and use contributor names, likenesses, voices, and images for hackathon publicity as described in the official rules.

## Rule priority

If this digest, the Devpost plugin, an announcement, or other material conflicts with the official rules or hackathon website, the official rules and website govern.
