# Naming and mission decisions

Updated: 2026-07-13

## Current strategic decisions

- Professional and team workflows only.
- Education/Learning mode removed.
- One three-stage happy path: Capture → Shape → Deliver.
- The whiteboard is the editable thinking layer and becomes the brief.
- Storyboard approval is required before video rendering.
- Coherent batch generation and exact style control are first-class requirements.
- The product should create its own Build Week demo package.

## Verdict on “ChatGPT Notes”

Do not use it as the product name.

1. It creates trademark and official-product confusion.
2. An adjacent Chrome extension already uses the name.
3. “Notes” describes storage, while this product moves from thought to delivery.
4. It undersells whiteboarding, coherent visual production, presentations, storyboards, and video.

## Approved project name

# WorkshopLM

**Tagline:** Turn raw thinking into finished work.

The name is intentionally legible beside NotebookLM during hackathon judging. It quickly communicates a grounded, source-aware workspace, while **Workshop** shifts the promise from passive notes and study outputs toward active professional shaping and production.

The product must not imply affiliation with Google or describe itself as an official OpenAI product. The pitch should establish the distinction immediately:

> NotebookLM helps you understand sources. WorkshopLM helps your team turn grounded thinking into a coherent package of finished work.

The shorter judge-facing formulation is:

> WorkshopLM is NotebookLM for finished work—built on the OpenAI API.

“NotebookLM, but better and with OpenAI” is useful internal shorthand. Public materials should replace the unbounded word “better” with the specific, demonstrated advantages.

Current discovery checks found no exact-name product, GitHub repository, or npm package. Neither `workshoplm.com` nor `workshoplm.ai` resolved when checked on 2026-07-13. These are useful hackathon signals, not legal trademark or domain-availability clearance.

## Rejected names

- **Notedex:** rejected. A current cross-platform note-card product already uses **NoteDex**, including handwriting, audio transcription, AI summarization, canvases, and storyboards. The collision is both exact and adjacent.
- **Notex:** rejected. **NoteX** is already an AI meeting-intelligence product that turns voice conversations into structured, searchable knowledge. The collision is adjacent to the proposed capture workflow.
- **Evidence Studio:** accurate but too compliance-oriented for the speed and creative-production promise.

## Current collision research

The first obvious naming territory is unusually crowded:

- **BriefCraft** already names several AI brief-generation products, including one that turns meetings, Slack threads, and email into creative briefs.
- **SignalCraft** already names multiple AI and marketing products.
- **ShapeWork** is an existing workflow-design practice.
- **TraceWork** names existing field-service and accounting software.
- **SourceFrame** is an existing source-grounded research-brief product.
- **DraftLoom** is already used for AI publishing and a scattered-notes-to-outlines product.
- **Contexture**, **Formant**, and **Groundline** are active AI or software brands.
- **Threadform** has a long-running canvas-products business, an occupied `.com`, and a current game using the same name.

These checks are product-discovery research, not trademark clearance. Do not select a public name without an exact-name web, app-store, domain, and trademark review.

## Naming implementation rule

Use **WorkshopLM** in the product design, demo, and Devpost submission. Use a replaceable lowercase internal package slug so a later legal or domain review does not require changing domain contracts. Do not use OpenAI, ChatGPT, GPT, Google, or NotebookLM marks in WorkshopLM's logo or imply endorsement.

## Recommended mission

> Help serious teams turn raw thinking into finished work before momentum disappears.

## Recommended vision

> A professional can speak an idea, shape it spatially, and deliver a complete on-brand asset package from one grounded workspace.

## Recommended aspiration

Become the thought-to-delivery layer for professional knowledge work: the place where conversation becomes structure, structure becomes creative direction, and creative direction becomes finished media.

## Product promise

> Start with your voice. Leave with the brief, deck, visuals, storyboard, and video.

## Positioning line

> The professional thought-to-delivery studio.

## Remaining founder decisions

1. Whether the primary artifact package is called a campaign kit, delivery package, or production kit.
2. How much human approval the demo exposes versus running as a single guided production flow.
