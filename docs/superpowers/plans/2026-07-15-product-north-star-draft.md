# Product north star — professional send-it bar

Date: 2026-07-15

Status: strategic reference; the deck-quality target is promoted into `GOAL.md`, while the broader product plan remains subject to evidence and sequencing.

## North star

A working professional turns messy inputs—meetings, documents, and half-formed thinking—into a deliverable they ship under their own name, faster than their current workflow, without lowering their standards.

NotebookLM helps people understand. WorkshopLM helps professionals ship.

The product lives or dies on output quality. Pipeline capability matters only when the resulting work clears the professional question: **Would I send this?**

## Wedge

- Primary user: a consultant, strategist, or enablement lead who produces client-facing or leadership-facing deliverables every week.
- Hero deliverable: a grounded, branded, source-defensible deck.
- Supporting outputs, in order: infographic, in-brand diagrams and section art, Storyboard, and Video.
- Empty-state promise: “From your meetings and documents to a deck you can defend, with every claim traced to its source.”

## First-session bar

- A cold professional understands the product in 20 seconds without narration.
- The first magic moment happens within two minutes: Sources enter and the live model visibly organizes the Map.
- The first shippable artifact is possible within 15 minutes from the professional's own material.
- No concept appears before the user can act on it.
- Test both the teaching fixture and a truly empty Workshop.

## Deliverable bar

### Deck

- Use real layout variety selected by content type rather than repeating one template.
- Make exact Style tokens improve the work, not merely satisfy a schema.
- Keep citations present, legible, and unobtrusive.
- Provide an editable export that survives normal PowerPoint-world handoff.
- Iterate against real external content until a senior client or leadership audience is a credible destination.

### Images

- Prioritize diagrams, concept visuals, and in-brand section art that belong in the hero deck.
- Evaluate the first real GPT Image 2 batch against “Would this go in a client deck?”
- Adjust the prompt and coherence system from inspected results, not from manifests alone.

### Video

- Hold pacing, narration, and visual coherence to a leadership- or LinkedIn-ready bar.
- Preserve the approved Storyboard and visible AI-voice disclosure.

### Every output

- Preserve one-interaction source trace.
- Support the professional's final 10% of editing either inside WorkshopLM or through a normal editable export.

## Trust is the product

- Any factual claim traces to its exact source sentence using the same gesture everywhere.
- Staleness is calm and actionable: identify what needs updating and offer one regeneration action.
- Brief and Storyboard approvals feel like consequential professional sign-offs.
- Unverified material and AI-generated voice remain honestly labeled.

## Return-visit bar

- Adding a new Source to an existing Workshop refreshes the Map and honestly stales dependent work.
- A second Workshop starts effortlessly.
- Style Libraries carry across Workshops so a brand is configured once.
- The weekly rhythm works: new meeting, refreshed Brief, regenerated deck, same Style, intact trace.

## Reality tests

1. **Dogfood zero:** WorkshopLM creates its own submission; log every point of friction as a product defect.
2. **Dogfood one:** create a genuinely external deliverable for a real audience that does not grade on effort.
3. **Cold professionals:** observe three to five representative people using an empty Workshop with their own material and no coaching.
4. **Graduation:** one person outside the project uses WorkshopLM twice without being prompted.

## Kill list

Education mode, general Canva scope, hosted multi-user infrastructure, every connector, autonomous publishing, and consistency claims without inspected proof remain out of scope.

## Hackathon role

Build Week is a deadline and distribution event. The submission is dogfood zero, not the product's final definition. Recording critiques should grade the outputs against the send-it bar as well as the judging rubric. Dogfood one begins immediately after submission.
